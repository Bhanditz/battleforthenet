    The Federal Communications Commission is set on repealing Title II Net Neutrality rules — a move that would disproportionately harm people of color.

    In the face of systemic racism and bias, the open internet has created new pathways for self-expression, entrepreneurship, political participation, education, employment, housing, healthcare, community organizing and so much more.

    What is Net Neutrality?

    Net Neutrality is the basic principle that protects our free speech on the internet. Title II of the Communications Act provides the legal foundation for Net Neutrality and prevents internet service providers from slowing down or blocking websites, or from charging apps and sites extra fees to reach an audience.
